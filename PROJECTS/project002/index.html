Petit Gateau

Ingredients:

  • 200 grams of semi-sweet chocolate
  • 2 tablespoons of unsalted butter
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 egg yolks
  • Half a cup of sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour

Preparation:

  1. Melt 200 grams of semi-sweet chocolate and 2 tablespoons of unsalted butter in a double boiler. Reserve.
  2. In a mixer, add 2 eggs, 2 egg yolks, and half a cup of sugar. Beat until you get a light cream.
  3. Then, add the chocolate mixture, 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour and mix.
  4. Transfer the mixture to buttered and floured molds.
  5. Bake in a preheated oven at 200 degrees Celsius for 6 to 10 minutes.

Now unmold while still hot and serve with vanilla ice cream.

Lemon Meringue Pie

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of wheat flour
  • 3 tablespoons of sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 4 tablespoons of margarine
  • 1 can of condensed milk
  • 1 can of heavy cream
  • Half a cup of lemon juice
  • 1 sachet of Dr. Oetker unflavored gelatin powder
  • 3 tablespoons of warm water

Preparation:

    • Dough
  1. In a bowl, mix the dough ingredients until smooth.
  2. Roll out the dough into a circle larger than the diameter of a removable-bottom pan.
  3. Line the bottom and sides of the pan with the dough and prick it with a fork. Bake in a preheated oven at 180 degrees Celsius for 15 minutes.
    • Filling
  4. Dissolve the Dr. Oetker unflavored gelatin powder in water and hydrate for 15 seconds in the microwave or in a water bath.
  5. In a blender, mix sweetened condensed milk, lime juice, heavy cream, lime zest, and the dissolved and hydrated gelatin until smooth.
  6. Pour the filling over the baked crust.
    • Meringue
    • Whip the egg whites until stiff, gradually add the sugar, and continue beating until firm.
    • Spread over the filling, making peaks with the back of a spoon, and bake in a preheated oven at 180 degrees Celsius for 9 minutes.
    • Finish with lemon zest and serve immediately.

Ratatouille

Ingredients:

  • 250 grams of tomato passata
  • 1 eggplant
  • 1 zucchini
  • 3 tomatoes
  • 1 yellow bell pepper
  • 1 red bell pepper
  • A few sprigs of thyme
  • 50 grams of freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • olive oil
  • salt
  • black pepper

Preparation:

  1. Grease a baking dish with a drizzle of olive oil. Spread the tomato passata and season with salt and black pepper;
  2. Slice all the vegetables about 3 millimeters thick;
  3. Place the vegetable slices, alternating them to create a beautiful color pattern, around the dish. After completing the first round, move to the center and continue until the entire dish is lined;
  4. Season with salt, pepper, thyme leaves, and a drizzle of olive oil.
  5. Sprinkle with grated cheese and bake in the oven for approximately 40 minutes at 180 degrees Celsius, until the vegetables are tender and the cheese is golden. Serve immediately.

Coxinha

Ingredients:

    1. Filling
  • Water
  • 2 chicken bouillon cubes
  • 1 chicken breast
  • Onion to taste
  • Garlic to taste
  • Parsley to taste
  • Chives to taste
  • Rosemary to taste
    1. Bake
  • 2 cups of chicken cooking water
  • 1 tablespoon of margarine
  • 2 cups of wheat flour
    1. Assembly
  • 2 beaten eggs
  • 1 cup of breadcrumbs

Preparation:

    • Filling
  1. In a pot, add enough water to cook the chicken. Add 2 chicken bouillon cubes and 1 chicken breast.
  2. Cook until the chicken breast is tender
  3. Then, use two forks to shred the chicken breast.
  4. Add the shredded chicken to the pot and sauté with onion and garlic to taste.
  5. After that, season the shredded chicken with parsley, green onions, and rosemary. Set aside.
    • Bake
  6. In a pot, add 2 cups of chicken cooking water and 1 tablespoon of margarine. Bring to a boil.
  7. Then, add 2 cups of all-purpose flour. Stir quickly to avoid lumps.
  8. Let it cool and set aside.
    • Assembly
  9. Take the dough and make small balls.
  10. Then, flatten the dough in your hand and add the filling.
  11. Close the dough and shape it into a coxinha.
  12. Next, dip the coxinha in beaten egg and then in bread crumbs.
  13. Fry in hot oil. Using a slotted spoon, remove the coxinha and let it drain to get it nice and dry.
